1. Lviv National Opera House
One of the most magnificent buildings in all of Ukraine, the Lviv Opera was completed in 1900 and modeled after the Vienna State Opera. Its neo-Rococo facade, ornate interior, and world-class performances make it the crown jewel of the city's cultural life.

2. Rynok Square (Market Square)
The beating heart of Lviv's UNESCO-listed old town, Rynok Square is surrounded by 44 grand merchant houses dating back to the Renaissance and Baroque periods. The square is lined with cafés, fountains, and the iconic 14th-century Town Hall tower.

3. Dominican Cathedral
Built in the 18th century in a stunning Baroque style, the Dominican Cathedral is considered one of the finest examples of Baroque architecture in Eastern Europe. Its dramatic elliptical dome dominates the Lviv skyline and the richly decorated interior leaves visitors in awe.

4. High Castle Hill (Vysokyi Zamok)
The ruins of a 14th-century castle sit atop the highest hill in the city, offering sweeping panoramic views over Lviv's rooftops, towers, and green hills. It's the best vantage point to appreciate the city's remarkable architectural tapestry all at once.

5. Lychakiv Cemetery
Dating back to 1786, Lychakiv is one of the oldest and most atmospheric cemeteries in Europe, often compared to Père Lachaise in Paris. It's an open-air museum of elaborate tombstones, sculptures, and mausoleums where poets, scientists, and national heroes are buried among ancient oaks.
